Development of Elmer was part of a Finnish national CFD programme coordinated by the Technology Development Centre of Finland (TEKES) since 1995. The main objective was to develop a fluid dynamics program, based on the Finite Element Method (FEM), which could be utilized in industrial processes where heat transfer in different forms and incompressible flows are present.

Elmer was build as a collection of programmes for preprocessing, solving and postprocessing rather than a single monolithic application, i.e., each phase of the computational process can be handled separately. The aim was to develop a flexible tool in a sense that an engineer can work on a simulation on his own desktop on smaller scale before performing the full simulation on an appropriate machine.

Elmer was originally developed in co-operation between

  • CSC - Scientific Computing Ltd.
  • Helsinki University of Technology
  • Okmetic Ltd.
  • Technical Research Centre of Finland
  • University of Jyväskylä

Previous research on mesh generation, FEM methodology and FEM applications to flow problems, direct and iterative solvers and parallel computing in these institutes was utilized in the work.
